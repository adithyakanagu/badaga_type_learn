Founder of Badugu Bare (Badugu Script)

Badaga (Badugu) is an ancient language, the birth of it is unknown. Extensive studies by eminent anthropologists and linguists have time and again said that Badaga predates many of the established languages of India. For the Badagas (Badugarul), it is "DHEVARU BAASHE", the language of the Gods.

It was the year 1968 when a young Badugu boy living with his maternal grandmother, studying 8th standard at a government run school was restlessly questioning himself as to why there was no script for his mother tongue. In three years, he came out with a basic version of the Badugu script and started distributing it to his classmates.

In 1978, he came out with a final refined structure of the script. In 1991, he started teaching the script to twelve enthusiastic students including one non-Badugu student. Between 1978 and 1991, he developed the Badugu Grammar and a Badugu–Badugu–English dictionary.

Today, this gentleman, Mr. Yogesh R. of Kadasoley Village in Thodhanadu Seeme, without any fanfare, continues teaching the script to all interested people thereby inspiring generations of Badugaru. The entire credit for developing the Badugu script goes to this son of the soil.

Badaga Language Promotion Group (BLPG) is a voluntary organisation founded on 12-12-2012. Its vision is to regain the lost glory of the Badugu language and promote cultural renaissance by spreading usage of the Badugu script worldwide.

BLPG proudly presented the Badugu Script and 1st Level Text Book on 26-12-2012 at Beragini Village with the blessings of Hethal Amma.
